🗺️ The build order

The path,
not the cliff.

Three waves. Each one ships something useful on its own, and earns the right to build the next. You feel value in Wave 1, long before the flagship lands. Nobody has to wait for the whole vision to arrive before the app starts giving you time back.

We sequence by what is cheap and certain first, and what is ambitious and involved last. Same thesis throughout: AI gives the coach reach, it does not replace the coach.

The shape of it

Three waves, in order

Wave 1 · Now

Capture & Assist

Put your coaching into the app, and let it start carrying the after-hours questions and the blank-message-box problem right away.

What ships

  • A coach knowledge base with a simple capture flow, so your drills, cues, progressions and rules of thumb live in the app. Pillar 01 →
  • An athlete coach-companion in chat that answers from that knowledge, in your voice, around the clock.
  • A coach-side draft-reply assistant so you never start a message from nothing. Pillar 02 →

Why this is first

  • It rides entirely on the chat that already works in the app today. No new plumbing for athletes to learn.
  • It is the lowest technical risk of the three waves.
  • It pays off immediately, on day one, in your daily messages.
  • Everything later depends on the knowledge base existing, so this has to come first anyway.

Athlete feels

Asks a question at 9pm, gets a real answer at 9pm, in a voice that sounds like their coach, not a search engine.

Coach feels

Opens the app to find replies already drafted and the same beginner questions already handled. You edit and approve instead of typing from scratch.

Rough build scope
  • An AI service layer: a Firebase function that calls an LLM, so no keys or model logic ever sit on a phone.
  • A knowledge store plus grounding (RAG) so answers are pulled from your material, not invented.
  • A new chat message author (the companion) added to the existing chat model.
  • A persona config that captures your tone and standards.

Detail in the technical design.

Unlocks: the knowledge floor that Wave 2 builds on, and the trust that makes athletes happy to let the app draft for them.

Wave 2 · Next

Generate & Act

Now that the app knows your coaching, let it assemble the busywork: full workouts drafted from your library, assignments handled, every decision still yours.

What ships

  • Workout generation from your own drill library. "Build Marcus a 3-week shooting block" and a draft appears, made of your drills.
  • Assignment actions. "Assign yesterday's workout to the U16s" and it is staged for one tap.
  • Human-in-the-loop approval on everything that touches an athlete. Pillar 03 →

Why this is second

  • It needs the knowledge base from Wave 1 to draft anything that sounds like you.
  • It leans on the structured workout and drill data the app already stores.
  • Higher stakes: it writes athlete-facing things, so the approval and audit safety net has to be built before it can be trusted.

Athlete feels

Programs arrive faster and more often, tuned to what they actually asked for, never generic.

Coach feels

The blank-workout-builder is gone. You shape and approve instead of assembling drill by drill. Your library becomes a creative engine.

Rough build scope
  • Tool use / function calling so the AI can propose concrete app actions, not just text.
  • An action allow-list: a fixed menu of things the app is permitted to draft or stage.
  • An approval and preview UX so nothing reaches an athlete without your tap.
  • An audit log recording what was suggested, approved, and sent.

Detail in the technical design.

Unlocks: the habit of the app acting on your behalf safely, which is exactly the trust Wave 3 needs when it starts flagging videos for your attention.

Wave 3 · North star

See & Correct

The flagship. The app watches the reps athletes already film, gives instant feedback on the spot, and sends only the clips that really need your eyes to the top of your queue.

What ships

  • On-device pose and action detection on form videos: the body is tracked right on the athlete's phone.
  • Instant per-rep feedback and encouragement: "great depth, hold the top", "knees caving on the landing".
  • A coach triage queue that surfaces the few clips that genuinely need you, instead of all of them. Pillar 04 →

Why this is last

  • It is the most technically involved: real on-device ML, tuned movement by movement.
  • It carries the highest privacy bar: on-device by design, and often young athletes. How we handle it →
  • It is best built on top of the coaching knowledge from the earlier waves, so feedback reflects your standards, not a generic textbook.

Athlete feels

Films a set and gets a coach-like response in seconds, every single rep, even at 11pm in the driveway.

Coach feels

Your video queue stops being a wall of twelve clips. The app already gave the easy feedback and flagged the two that need a human. You spend your attention where it changes outcomes.

Rough build scope
  • On-device pose model integration (ML Kit or MediaPipe) so raw video stays on the phone.
  • Movement-by-movement form rules, starting narrow with 2-3 movements (squat, jump shot, landing) and widening from there.
  • Triage and flagging so coach attention is rationed to the clips that need it.
  • Privacy and consent flows, treated as a core feature. See pillar 04 →

Detail in the technical design.

Unlocks: the experience that makes Level Up unmistakable, an eye on every rep, grounded in everything captured in Waves 1 and 2.

Honest groundwork

What we need before Wave 1

A handful of decisions and a little setup come before the first feature. None of it is scary, but it is real, and naming it now keeps the build honest.

🤖 Pick the model

Which LLM powers the companion. This drives quality, cost and where data goes. A recommendation lives in the technical design.

🔌 The AI service layer

A small backend (a Firebase function) that every AI feature calls through. Build it once, reuse it for all three waves.

💸 Cost & rate limits

AI calls cost money per use. We set sensible per-user limits from day one so a bill never surprises us.

📚 Seed the knowledge

A short session with Benson to load the first real drills, cues and progressions. The companion is only as good as what we put in.

🛡️ Safety & moderation

Light guardrails so the companion stays in its lane: coaching, encouragement, the app. Not medical advice, not off-topic chat.

🎯 Define "your voice"

A first pass at the persona: how you talk, what you would never say, your standards. We refine it as athletes use it.

The reassuring part

Almost none of this is greenfield. The chat, the workouts, the videos, the user roles are all already in the app. The groundwork above is mostly choices and a thin new backend, not a rebuild. We are adding a brain to a body that already moves.

The throughline

Start where it is cheap and certain. End where it is ambitious and unforgettable.

Every wave gives you time back and gives athletes coaching they would not otherwise get. That is the only test, and all three waves pass it.
